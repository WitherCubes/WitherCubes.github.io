How do organisms reproduce

Reproduction

  • The ability of an organism to produce offspring or young ones or new organism is known as reproduction
  • The process by which living organism produce offspring of their own kind is known as reproduction
  • Each organism has certain life span.

Life Span

  • The time period between birth to death is called life span.

Examples in animals:

  • May Fly (1 day)
  • Tortoise (300 - 400 years)
  • Bow Head Whale (150 - 200 years)
  • Cockacoos, Amazon Parrots (50 - 70 years)

Examples in plants

  • Wolfia (2 weeks)
  • Pinus (300 years)
  • Rice (3 - 7 weeks)

Variation

Difference between parents and offspring is called variation.

Ex:

  • Color of the eye
  • External size of the ear (pinna)
  • External size of nose

Fission

Binary Fission

Process of one parent cell dividing to produce two daughter cells is known as binary fission.

Ex:

  • Amoeba
  • Paramecium
  • Bacteria

Multiple Fission

Process of nucleus dividing to many daughter cells is known as multiple fission.

Ex:

  • Plasmodium
  • Few algae
  • Few bacteria

Budding

Exogeneous Budding

Exogeneous budding is a type of asexual reproduction. In this process a new organism develops as a form of an outgrowth on a bud on the surface of the mother plant.

Ex:

  • Budding in Hydra

  • Budding in Yeast

Endogeneous Budding

Endogeneous budding is the process when th bud develops inside the parent body.

Ex:

  • Freshwater sponge (Spongilla)

Spore Formation

Zoospore

A zooospore is a spore that is motile, biflagellated, prereleased in water

Ex:

  • Chlamydomonas

Conidum

Conidia are non motile spores that are endogenous spores in penicillium. Endogenous spores are found in rhizopus.

Fragmentation

The process of asexual reporoduction in spirogyra is known as fragmentation, where the body of spirogyra when mature breaks into many small pieces (or) fragments due to storng water currents. These various pieces will later grow into new induviduals.

Regeneration

Planaria in either favourable or unfavourable conditions produce many pieces. Each piece gives rise to complete organism (missing part of body regenerates).

Ex:

  • Planaria

Plasmotomy

Cytokianasis first, followed by canyokinasis.

Ex:

  • Multi nuclear protozoans
  • Opalina
  • Pleomyxa
