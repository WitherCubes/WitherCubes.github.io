Chemical equation

The symbolic representation of a chemical change by using their formula’s is called chemical equation.

$$Mg(s) + O_2(g) \implies \frac{Δ}{}\implies MgO(s) + Heat$$

  • In the above reaction the total atoms of each element is not equal to the both reactants and products, hence that is called unbalanced (or) skeletal chemical equation.

$$Total\ Mass\ of\ reactants\ ≠\ Total\ Mass\ of\ products$$

Balanced Chemical Equations

All the chemical changes followed by law of conservation of mass which was given by Lavoiser.

According to this law

$$Mass\ of\ reactants\ =\ Mass\ of\ products$$ $$\quad (or) \quad$$ $$The\ total\ number\ of\ atoms\ of\ same\ element\ in\ reactants\ and\ products\ is\ always\ same$$

Example: $$2Mg + O_2 \implies 2MgO$$ $$Mg = 2 \qquad Mg = 2$$ $$O = 2 \qquad O =2$$

Q1: The reaction between Sodium Sulphate and Barium Chloride to form Sodium Chloride and white precipitate of Barium Sulphate, write the balanced chemical equation

Ans: $$Na_2SO_4(aq) + BaCl_2(aq) \implies 2NaCl(aq) + BaSO_4(s)\downarrow$$

Symbols used in chemical equations

  • $$\uparrow \implies Evolving\ of\ gas$$
  • $$\downarrow \implies Formation\ of\ precipitate\ (Insoluble\ Solid)$$
  • $$Δ \implies Heat,\ hν \implies Sunlight$$
  • $$ΔH = -ve \implies Exothermic,\ ΔH = +ve \implies Endothermic$$
  • $$N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \implies \frac{Fe}{}\implies 2NH_3(g),\ in\ here\ Fe\ is\ a\ catalyst$$

Catalyst

Catalyst is a chemical substance which increases the rate of chemical reaction without involving itself.

Ex:

  • In hydrogenation of oils Raney Nickel (powdered Ni) catalyst is used.

Balancing equation gives information about

  • Physical state of reactants and products.
  • Gases evolving (or) precipitation formation.
  • Nature of bonds present in reaction’s products.
  • Reaction Conditions i.e temperature, pressure, catalyst, etc.
  • Mole concept related ideas i.e amount of reactants and products, volume evolving which we can collect from balancing equation.
  • Exothermic (or) Endothermic.
  • We can predict the type of chemical reactions.

Example 1:

$$CaCO_3(s) \implies \frac{Δ}{}\implies Cao(s) + CO_2 \uparrow$$

  • Obeys Law of conservation of mass
  • Reactants: Calcium Carbonate = Solid; Products: Calcium Oxide = Solid, Carbon dioxide = gas
  • Happened in presence of heat
  • Endothermic reaction
  • Chemical decomposition reaction
  • 22.4 litres of Carbon dioxide is evolved
  • 100g of Calcium Carbonate undergoes decompositionwhich gives 56g of Calcium Oxide and 44g of Carbon dioxide

Example 2:

$$N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \implies \frac{Fe}{}\implies 2NH_3(g) + heat$$

  • Obeys Law of conservation of mass
  • Both Reactants and products are in gaseous state
  • In this reaction Iron is used as catalyst
  • Chemical combination reaction
  • 1 mole Nitrogen reacts with 3 moles of Hydrogen which produces 2 moles of Ammonia
  • Exothermic reaction

Q6: To produce 5.6g of molten Iron how much amount of Aluminium is required? $$Fe_2O_3 + Al \implies Al_2O_3 + Fe + heat$$

Ans:

Based on the above equation $$Fe_2O_3 + 2Al \implies Al_2O_3 + 2Fe + heat$$

  • 2 moles of Al —> 2 moles Fe
  • 2 x 27g of Al —> 2 x 56g of Fe
  • 54g Al —> 112g Fe
  • n = 5.6g Fe
  • n x 112g = 54 x 5.6
  • n = 54 x 5.6 / 112
  • n = 2.7g

Q7: The reaction between Hydrogen and Chlorine forms Hydrogen Chloride, then calculate the following

1) If 0.1 moles of Hydrogen consumed the how many grams of HCl is formed?

Ans:

$$H_2 + Cl_2 \implies 2HCl$$ $$1\ mole\ H_2 \implies 2\ HCl$$ $$1\ mole\ H_2 \implies 2 \times 36.5g$$ $$0.1\ mole \implies x$$ $$x = 73 \times 0.1$$ $$x = 7.3g$$

2) If 11.2 litres of Chlorine gas is consumed, then how many grams of Hydrogen Chloride is formed?

Ans:

$$1\ mole\ Cl_2 \implies 2\ HCl$$ $$22.4\ litres \implies 2 \times 22.4\ litres$$ $$11.2\ litres \implies x$$ $$x = 22.4\ litres$$
