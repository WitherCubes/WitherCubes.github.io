Light

Optics

It’s a branch of physics which describes about phenomenon and laws associated with generation and propagation of light, its interaction with the matter called optics.

Optics are classified into two types

  • Ray Optics
  • Physical Optics

Ray Optics

It treats the propagation of light in terms of rays and is valid if only the wavelength of the light is much smaller than the size of the obstacle.

Ray of light

It is a direction of light in which it travels. a = size of obstacle λ = wave length of light

a » λ

Beam of light

A bundle or bunch of light rays is called beam of light.

Parallel beam of light

All the rays move parallel to each other.

Convergent beam of light

All the rays move towards a single point.

Divergent beam of light

All the rays emerge from a single point.

Production of electromagnetic waves

  • These waves are produced by oscillating charges which produces mutually perpendicular oscillating electric and magnetic fields.
  • These waves are transverse waves in nature.
  • The speed of an electromagnetic wave doesn’t depend on its frequency and wave length.

Properties of light

  • Light travels in a straight line.
  • Speed of light in vacuum is 3 x 10^8 m/s 3 X 10 power 8 m/s.
  • Speed of light is considerably reduces in glass and water.
  • It doesn’t require any medium to propagate.
  • The wavelength of visible light is 4 x 10^-7 m to 7 x 10^-7 m 4 X 10 power -7 m/s to 7 X 10 power -7 m/s.
    • 0.4 μm to 0.7 μm 1 μm = 10 power -6 m.
    • 400 nm to 700 nm 1 nm = 10 power 9 m.
  • Light doesn’t contain any particle but it carries energy and momentum.
  • Light is supposed to have massless particles called photons.
  • It is an electromagnetic wave and it is transverse in nature.

Reflection of light

  • The process of sending back light from its surface is called reflection of light.
  • The bouncing back of light rays to the same medium which falls on the surface of an object

Laws of reflection of light

  • Incident ray, Reflected ray, and Normal meet at the Point of incidence on the same plane.
  • ∠i = ∠r Angle of Incident ray = Angle of Reflected ray.
  • ∠Gi = ∠Gr Angle of Glancing angle of incidence = Angle of Glancing angle of reflection.

Angle of Incidence

The angle between incident ray and normal ray is the angle of incidence.

Angle of reflection

The angle between reflection ray and normal ray is the angle of reflection.

Case 1

  • If an angle of incidence is 0, then the angle of reflection is 0.
  • That means if a ray is incident normally on a boundary or surface after reflection, it retraces its path.

Case 2

  • None of the frequency, wavelength, and speed changes but amplitude and intensity of the reflected ray are slightly less than that of incident ray as some part of energy is absorbed at the surface.

Case 3

  • On smooth, plain boundary a parallel beam of light incident, it will remain parallel even after reflection. This reflection is known as regular or specular reflection. (a)
  • If the boundary is uneven or irregular, the parallel beam of light is reflected in random directions. This reflection is known as irregular or diffused reflection. (b)

Angle of deviation

  • 180° = i + r + δ
  • δ = 180° - (i + r)
  • δ = 180° - 2i (or) 180° - 2r [∵ i = r]

  • The angle between extended incident ray and reflected ray is called angle of deviation.

Real object

In this case incident rays are diverging and the point of divergence is the position of real object.

Virtual Object

In this case incident rays are converging and the point of convergence is the position of virtual object.

Real Image

This image is formed by the actual intersection of light rays. It can be obtained on a screen and inverted.

Virtual Image

It is formed when the light rays appear to be originatin from a point but doesn’t meet actually and is erect.
