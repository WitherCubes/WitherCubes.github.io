Personification:

Metaphor:

  • Fog is compared to a cat (On little cat’s feet).

Questions:

1) What does Sandburg think the fog is like?

Ans: He thinks the fog is a thick cloud of tiny water droplets present in the atmosphere.

2) How does the fog come?

Ans: The fog comes mostly silent, that no one knows how the fog fog enters the city, he also says that the fog comes like a cat that comes on its little feet.

3) What does ‘it’ in the third line refer to?

Ans: It refers to both the fog and the cat.

4) What does the poet actually say that the fog is like aa cat? Find 3 things that tells us that the fog is like a cat.

Ans: Yes, the poet says that the fog is like a cat in certain ways like: - Fog advances towrards the city very calmly like a cat. - Similarly the fog silently covers the whole city and the harbour and sits over them looking around like a cat. - When he says that the fog sits over the whole city like how a cat would sit and silently come in and out of the house.
