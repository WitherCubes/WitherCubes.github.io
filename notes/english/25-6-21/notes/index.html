Fire and Ice Class Notes 1

June 25, 2021 - By Sanjay Pavan

Rhyme scheme:

  • ABAA, BCBCB

Theme:

  • Only love, equality, mutual understanding, sympathy for one and all can help in establishing peace on the earth.

Alliteration:

  • Some Say
  • Favour Fire
  • World Will

Enjambment:

  • From what I‚Äôve tasted of desire I hold with those who favour fire.

Symbolism:

  • Fire - Excessive desire, Excessive Passion which make people restless and violent.
  • Ice - DIslike, hated, coldness, in difference to one another, jealous.

Personification:

  • The poet personifies fire and ice by giving them mind and power to destroy anything.

Anaphora:

  • Some say

Imagery:

  • To say that for destruction, ice is also great.

Points:

  • The poet feels that the world of ours will come to an end.
  • On the basis of what people think, the poet believes that the world world would come to an end by two highly contrasting but powerful elements of nature - Fire and Ice.
  • The poet shows his argument with those who believe that fire will cause the end of the world.
  • Turns out that ice can also cause the world to end.
  • In the end the poet agrees that it doesn’t matter if it is fire or ice, both can cause the end of the world.
