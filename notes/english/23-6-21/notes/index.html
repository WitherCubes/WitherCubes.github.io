Dust Of Snow Class Notes 1

June 23, 2021

Meanings:

  • Preening - Cleaning
  • Rued - Held in regret

Rhyme scheme:

  • ABAB, CDCD

Theme:

  • Positive perspective

Alliteration:

  • Saved Some
  • Has given my heart

Enjambment:

  • This is used throughout the poem.

Inversion:

  • 1st paragraph

Irony:

  • Rued
  • Hemlock

Symbolism:

  • Hemlock Tree, Crow - Bad Omen, Sadness, Inauspicious things

Metaphors:

  • Huge mountains of clouds

Points:

  • Here the poet uses inauspicious things to bring joy and happiness.
  • One must not take things for granted and should be open and accept whatever nature choosen to give them.
  • The poet feels that he has wasted his past of the day and happy that the rest of his day is not wasted.
  • We can either think positively or negatively about the events, depending on our perspectives and point of view. However we should adopt the speaker’s outlook and maintain a positive perspective of life in general.

Q: Who was socrates?

  • Socrates was a Greek philosopher from Athens who is credited as a founder of Western philosophy and the first moral philosopher of the Western ethical tradition of thought. An enigmatic figure, Socrates authored no texts and is known mainly through the posthumous accounts of classical writers, particularly his students Plato and Xenophon.
